# Concentration risk

Concentration is how much of your portfolio a single stock, sector, or theme controls. It cuts both ways: it makes great years spectacular and bad years unrecoverable.

## Practical thresholds
- **Single stock**: above 15–20% of the portfolio, one earnings miss moves your whole net worth. Professional funds usually cap single names near 10%.
- **Single sector**: above 40–45%, you own a sector bet, not a portfolio. Sector cycles (IT 2022, PSU 2024) can retrace 30–50%.
- **Top-2 positions**: if two names are more than a third of your money, your "diversified portfolio" is really two decisions wearing a costume.

## The thesis test
Concentration is only defensible when it is deliberate. Ask: "Did I choose this weight, or did it drift here?" If a stock doubled and now dominates, you haven't been brave — you've been lazy about rebalancing.

## Fixing it
Trim oversized winners back to target weight, redirect fresh SIPs to underweight areas (cheaper than selling, no tax event), and rebalance on a calendar (quarterly), not on headlines.
