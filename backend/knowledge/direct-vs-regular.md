# Direct vs Regular mutual fund plans

Every mutual fund in India comes in two plans holding identical portfolios: **Direct** and **Regular**. Regular plans embed a distributor commission in the expense ratio — typically 0.5%–1.5% extra per year — paid to whoever "sold" you the fund. Direct plans skip the middleman entirely.

## Why it matters
On a ₹5,000/month SIP over 20 years, a 1% higher expense ratio costs roughly ₹8–12 lakh in lost compounding. Same fund, same manager, same stocks — the only difference is the fee leak.

## How to switch
1. You cannot "convert" units — you redeem the Regular units and purchase Direct units (or start a fresh Direct SIP and stop the Regular one).
2. Buy Direct on the AMC's own site or platforms like Kuvera, Zerodha Coin, and Groww (which sell Direct plans).
3. Watch exit load (usually nil after 1 year for equity funds) and capital gains tax on the redemption.
4. Takes about 5 minutes per fund.

## Red flag
If your fund name doesn't say "Direct" in it, you are paying a distributor — possibly one who did nothing beyond the original sale.
