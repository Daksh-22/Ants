# Mutual fund overlap

Owning several funds is not the same as being diversified. Most Indian large-cap funds hold the same 40–60 index heavyweights (HDFC Bank, Reliance, ICICI, Infosys, TCS...), so two "different" funds can share 30–70% of their portfolios by weight.

## Rules of thumb
- Overlap under 20%: genuinely complementary funds.
- 20–40%: acceptable if the styles differ (e.g. large-cap + small-cap).
- Above 40%: you're paying two expense ratios for one portfolio.

## Common overlap traps
- Two large-cap funds from different AMCs: typically 50–65% overlap.
- A flexi-cap plus a large-cap: usually 35–50%.
- An index fund plus any large-cap active fund: the active fund is mostly the index with extra fees.

## What to do
Pick one fund per role: one broad equity core (index or flexi-cap), one deliberate satellite (small-cap, international, or thematic), and stop. Check overlap before adding any new fund — if the new fund's top-10 looks like your existing fund's top-10, it isn't a new idea.
