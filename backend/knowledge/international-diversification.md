# International diversification for Indian investors

A 100%-India portfolio is a bet on one economy, one currency, and one regulator. India can compound beautifully for a decade and still have multi-year flat stretches (2010–2013). Global exposure is the cheapest hedge against a purely local decade.

## How much
A common target for Indian retail investors is 10–20% international, mostly US large-cap/tech, added gradually via SIP.

## Vehicles
- **International ETFs/FoFs on Indian exchanges** (e.g. NASDAQ-100 ETFs, FANG+ ETFs): simplest — rupee-denominated, no US brokerage, no LRS paperwork. Watch for premium-to-NAV when RBI overseas limits pause fresh subscriptions.
- **Direct US investing via LRS**: real ownership of US shares, but adds brokerage setup, forex conversion cost (~0.5–1.5%), and US estate-tax considerations above $60k.
- **Indian companies with global revenue** (IT services): partial hedge, but they still trade with the Indian market.

## Tax note
Since April 2023, international funds are taxed at slab rates if bought after that date (no LTCG indexation) — factor this in, but don't let tax kill diversification entirely.
