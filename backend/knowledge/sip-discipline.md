# SIP discipline and rupee-cost averaging

A SIP (systematic investment plan) automates buying a fixed amount on a fixed date. Its power is behavioral, not mathematical: it removes the decision, and the decision is where most investors lose.

## Why consistency beats timing
Missing the 10 best market days over 20 years roughly halves your final corpus — and the best days cluster next to the worst ones, exactly when scared money sits out. A SIP keeps you buying through the dip you'd never buy voluntarily.

## Streaks matter
An unbroken SIP streak through a drawdown is the single strongest predictor of long-term investor returns. Pausing SIPs in a correction is selling low with extra steps.

## Practical rules
- Date the SIP just after salary day. Money that doesn't sit in savings doesn't get spent.
- Step up the SIP 10% every year (or with every raise) — a ₹5,000 SIP stepped up 10%/yr ends ~65% larger over 15 years than a flat one.
- One SIP per fund role. Five SIPs into five overlapping funds is one SIP with paperwork.
